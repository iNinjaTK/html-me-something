My Internship Journey

Saint Louis Internship Program (SLIP)

My journey starts Junior Year of high school I'm halfway done with my high school career, and I've done more than enough community service hours to graduate. It was time for me to gain some real work experience, but customer service isn't what I have in mind. I see on my school's bulletin board of ads for scholarship programs and academic events a poster for an internship program simply called the Saint Louis Internship Program. After turning in an application and doing an interview, I was accepted, and after a 10-weekend training period, I was ready for my introduction to corporate life as an intern at:

  • Thompson Coburn
  • PARIC Corporation

Thompson Coburn - Summer 2012

This was the first company I was ever employed under. Thompson Coburn is a law firm with a location in the downtown area of St. Louis at the time. It's possible to call me a Legal Intern during this time, but there was only so much I can do as a high school intern who wasn't focused in studying law more so than he was just looking for a summer job. I perform all sorts of clerical duties you would expect a high school student can only do: sorting files, digitizing case archives, printing copies, etc. During my time here under the SLIP, I was given an opportunity to carry out a mock trial where I and a group of other law firm interns get together, choose sides, argue for sides in a court case, and determine who carried out their defense the best. That would be a highlight of my summer that year.

PARIC Corporation - Summer 2013

Another school year goes by. I graduate high school. I return to the SLIP to request for one last summer internship before heading off to college at Saint Louis University. PARIC Corporation was my assigned company for that year, which is a construction company that was responsible for many of the construction project I only started to learn about throughout the Saint Louis city and county areas. I continued performing my clerical duties throughout my time there rotating from department to department at the company. I was ready to finish what would have been my last summer internship before college when I suddenly received a call.

Emma Bowen Foundation

During my Senior Year of high school before I graduated and began my internship at PARIC, I applied for the Emma Bowen Foundation on a whim. A nationally-recognized program that targets college students interested in media and guarantees four summer internships upon acceptance? Count me in! Though I had little hope that I would get in, I received my acceptance call during my internship period at PARIC and they wanted me to start immediately?! Crazy! So I had to call the SLIP Program Directors and tell them the great news but also my unfortunate premature end to the internship at PARIC. My two-week notice was filed, and although they didn't like to hear it, my program directors were generous and understood how great the opportunity I had before me was. July of that summer would begin my first internship at Charter Communications.

  • IT Management - Summer 2013
  • Network Engineering - Summer 2014
  • Marketing and Creative Services - Summer 2015
  • Human Resources - Summer 2016
  • IT Management - Summer 2017

Charter Communications - IT Management - Summer 2013

After my time at PARIC, I moved on to Charter Communications as an IT intern. Though I still haven't started my college years yet, my new managers still wanted me to have some sort of foundational knowledge in tech and the management of the business process before starting school. During my time here, in addition to performing the usual clerical duties, I also took the time to study the software development life cycle and the business process management workflow, though I would never apply my knowledge until much later in my college years. At last, my Freshman Year of college at Saint Louis University begins.

Charter Communications - Network Engineering - Summer 2014

I was on a Computer Science craze Freshman year, taking as many computer science and programming classes as possible without any regard for my gen-ed classes like natural sciences or literature. I return to Charter for my second summer internship and I hit a slump. As a rotational program, they place me with the Network Engineering team I couldn't retain anything. Studying network connections had little meaning to me. In addition, my managers were too busy with their own projects to give me any work of my own other than generic reading assignments of network operations. I would spend the rest of that summer starting and finishing House M.D. during work hours (that's bad, don't do that).

Spectrum Reach - Marketing and Creative Services - Summer 2015

Sophomore Year came and went. Computer Science was progressing faster than I can retain and comprehend. I could no longer keep my grades up, and I saw a trend that was spiraling downward. I made a preemptive strike on my work ethic and switched to an IT Management major in the business school. Returning to Charter for a third time, I was more ready than ever to not repeat my mistakes from last year. Dabbing at my creative side and my minor in Film Studies, I spend a majority of my summer working with the marketing and creative services department that dealt with creating and displaying advertisements on Charter's TV networks. Though it was also somewhat slow, I felt I was making more progress and learning more this summer than last summer at the very least.

Charter Communications - Human Resources - Summer 2016

Junior Year flashed before my eyes. I was fast approaching Senior Year, and I was beginning to doubt my future. I started wanting to learn to code again, but the coding courses required for my major only dabbled in it. Just enough to give me a somewhat weak foundation in programming. My summer internship in Human Resources didn't help to quell my doubts either. However, I made the most of it. Operating more in email distribution and spreadsheet generation, I did anything I could to apply programming to the Excel spreadsheets I generated, whether it was as simple as short computation, to long form absolute references to specific cells. Either way, my Senior Year is coming fast, and, reflecting on my resume until now, I felt no closer to being ready for the workforce in IT post-graduation than I was last year.

Charter Communications - IT Management - Summer 2017

My four years of college are up. My four summer internships at Charter through the Emma Bowen Foundation are up. Anticipating a potential gap in work after graduation, I applied for a Charter summer internship through Charter's own website. Using my resources from the Emma Bowen Foundation and the managers I knew at Charter during my times there, I was able to return for one truly final internship with Charter in a department directly related to my career. During my time in the IT Intake team, I learned the process for taking in requests for application updates or changes. I learned how to solicit business requirements from requestors, and convey those thoughts into reality. Needless to say, I learned more about my field in this summer internship period than I did throughout my four-year rotational period during college. Although my team had no room to bring me on as a full-time employee, my hiring manager used her resources to help me network with Brooksource, a contracting company that specializes in placing recent college graduates in IT roles throughout Saint Louis. After countless trial and error with interviews, Brooksource successfully placed me at Express Scripts, where I currently am today. And hopefully, LaunchCode will be responsible for the next step in my career.
